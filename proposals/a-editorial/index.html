For trail & ultra runners

No one runs straight up a mountain.

Switchback writes adaptive training plans for trail and ultra runners. Each week your plan reshapes from what you actually ran — never too steep, never too fast — and pushes the workouts to your Garmin.

Built for trail & ultra · Pushes to Garmin · Adapts every week

The principle

The trail-runner's trick for steep climbs.

A switchback lets you climb terrain a straight line never could — by zigzagging, never exceeding the safe gradient. Your training plan should do the same.

Never exceed the safe gradient

Weekly load caps and recovery weeks are non-negotiable. The plan refuses to compound itself into injury.

Adjust the route as you climb

Every Sunday, last week's data — HR, pace, vert, sleep, your notes — feeds back in. Next week reshapes around what actually happened.

Reach the top gradually

Periodization that builds across months, not weeks. Base, build, peak, taper — the long arc that gets you to the start line whole.

How it works

Four steps. Then the loop.

  1. 01

    Describe your race

    "50k in the Alps, 3,200m vert, July 18th, I can train Mon/Wed/Fri/Sat, fastest 10k is 42 minutes." Plain language. No forms.

  2. 02

    Get a plan written for you

    Periodized block. Real coaching structure. Built around your zones, your schedule, your terrain.

  3. 03

    Workouts land on your Garmin

    Pushed straight to your Garmin Connect calendar. Your watch syncs automatically. HR targets, vert goals, repeat groups — all native.

  4. 04

    The plan rewrites itself every Sunday

    Last week's actual data plus your notes feed back in. Next week's switchback bends around what really happened — not what was supposed to.

Built for the steep end

Most plan apps stop at the marathon. We start there.

Vertical-first workouts

Hill repeats with vert targets, not just time. Long climbs measured in metres ascended.

Long days that look like the race

Back-to-backs. Hilly out-and-backs. Time-on-feet on terrain that matches the start line.

Effort-led where it matters

No HR alarm halfway up a steep climb. Effort cues on the descent. The plan knows when targets stop applying.

Zones that stay honest

Periodic AeT and LT drift tests keep your zones accurate as you get fitter. The plan adjusts the dials, not just the dose.

Why not Runna?

Runna is a great road app. We are not building a road app.

If you train for races with more than 1,000 metres of vert, technical terrain, or distances past 42 km, your plan needs to know that. Runna's doesn't. Switchback's does.

And: Runna picks a template and tweaks the paces. Switchback regenerates next week from your data. Both matter. The second one is the one nobody else does.

Built by someone training for one of these races

A 14-week training block. Then a product.

Switchback started as a personal tool for training for the Monte Rosa Walserwaeg Trail — 43 km, 3,200 metres of vert, UTMB World Series, July 2026. Every workout type, every adaptation rule, every Garmin integration detail was tested on a real training block before it became a product. By the time it shipped, the system had written, pushed, and rewritten 14 weeks of plans for one runner. Now it's ready for everyone else.

— Peter Van Hee, founder
