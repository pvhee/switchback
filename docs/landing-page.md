## How it works

**1. Describe your race.**
"50k in the Alps, 3,200m vert, July 18th, I can train Mon/Wed/Fri/Sat,
fastest 10k is 42 minutes." Plain language. No forms.

**2. Get a plan built around you.**
Periodized block, real coaching structure, written for your zones and
your schedule. Not a template with paces swapped in.

**3. Workouts land on your Garmin.**
Push to Garmin Connect calendar; your watch syncs automatically.
Threshold targets, vert goals, repeat groups, all native.

**4. The plan rewrites itself every Sunday.**
Last week's HR, pace, vert, sleep, and your own notes feed back in.
Next week reshapes around what actually happened — not what was
supposed to happen.

## Built for the underserved end of running

Most plan apps stop at the marathon. Switchback starts there.

- **Vertical-first workouts** — hill repeats with vert targets, not
  just time.
- **Long days that look like the race** — back-to-backs,
  out-and-backs on terrain that matches.
- **Effort-led when it should be** — no HR alarm on a steep climb,
  effort cues on a technical descent.
- **AeT and LT checks** — periodic drift tests so your zones stay
  honest as you get fitter.

## Why not just use Runna?

Runna is a great road app. We are not building a road app.

If you train for races that have more than 1,000m of vert, technical
terrain, or distances past 42km, your plan needs to know that. Runna's
doesn't. Switchback's does.

## Built by someone training for one of these races

Switchback started as a personal tool for training for the Monte Rosa
Walserwaeg Trail — 43km, 3,200m vert, UTMB World Series, July 2026.

detail was tested on a real training block before it became a product.

## Notes for the build

- **Above the fold must communicate**: it's for trail/ultra, it adapts
  weekly, it pushes to Garmin. Three things, no more.
- **The waitlist form is the conversion event.** Track sign-ups with
  Plausible / Fathom — avoid GA4 for both privacy and ToS reasons
  pre-launch.
- **Founder-built tone, not corporate fitness-brand tone.** Stay
  closer to how iRunFar / Trail Runner Mag write than how Strava
  writes.
- **One short founder photo / bio block** somewhere mid-page.
  Strengthens credibility for both visitors and Garmin reviewers.
- **Privacy + Terms links must be live** before submitting the Garmin
  application. They'll click them.
